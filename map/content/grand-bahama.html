Grand Bahama sits 55 miles off the Florida coast with deep blue water on three sides and flats that most anglers overlook completely. The West End is one of the best wahoo fisheries in the Bahamas, the south shore drops into serious yellowfin tuna water, and the east end has bonefishing that rivals the Out Islands. All of it is reachable from a single base on an island with real infrastructure.

Why Grand Bahama

Geography is doing the heavy lifting here. The Gulf Stream sweeps past the western tip at West End, bringing wahoo, sailfish, and dolphin within 20 minutes of the dock. South of Lucaya, the Little Bahama Bank drops off a shelf into 3,000-foot water where yellowfin tuna stack up on the temperature breaks. And the east end of the island, around Sweeting's Cay and Lightbourne Cay, has vast shallow flats that hold bonefish year-round without the guide pressure you'll find on Andros or Bimini.

Grand Bahama also has the airports, the marinas, the grocery stores, and the rental cars that make logistics easy. You can fly into Freeport, grab a car, and be fishing within an hour of landing. That combination of serious fish and simple access is hard to beat.

How to Fish It

Offshore - West End

West End is wahoo country. The Gulf Stream pushes hard past the point, creating current edges and temperature breaks that wahoo patrol from November through April. High-speed trolling at 14-18 knots with Yo-Zuri Bonita lures, Rapala Magnums, or rigged horse ballyhoo on wire is the standard play. Moon phases matter: the three days before and after the full moon produce the best bites.

Sailfish work the same edges, especially January through April. Slow-trolling live goggle-eyes or rigged ballyhoo on 20-30 lb class tackle picks them up. Blue marlin pass through in summer, but this is primarily a wahoo and sailfish zone.

Offshore - South Shore

The deep water south of Lucaya and Gold Rock holds yellowfin tuna, blackfin tuna, and the occasional bigeye. Chunking with butterflied bonito or squid on 50 lb stand-up tackle works the deeper fish. Trolling cedar plugs and small skirted lures at the color changes picks up yellowfin feeding on the surface. Best months: December through May, with March and April producing the biggest yellowfin.

Flats - East End

The east end flats around Sweeting's Cay, Lightbourne Cay, and the creeks north of McLean's Town hold excellent bonefishing. Average fish run 3-5 lbs, with regular shots at 7-8 lb fish on the deeper edges. These flats are less pressured than the western Bahamas, and the fish tend to be less skittish.

Fish incoming tide when the bones push onto the sand and turtle grass flats to feed. Wade fishing is productive on the harder bottom areas. Fly anglers throw #4-6 Gotcha or Crazy Charlie patterns on 7-8 wt rods. Spin anglers use 1/8 oz jigs with fresh or artificial shrimp on 8-10 lb line. Barracuda hang in the deeper channels between flats and will cut you off if you run too light on leader.

Reef Fishing

The reef system running along the south shore holds yellowtail snapper, mutton snapper, and grouper (in season). Drifting with cut ballyhoo or squid on light conventional tackle produces steady action. Peterson Cay National Park has healthy reef structure in 20-40 feet that holds fish year-round.

When to Go

  • November-January: Wahoo season opens strong at West End. Bonefish concentrated on east end flats. Cool, comfortable weather.
  • February-April: Peak wahoo and sailfish. Yellowfin tuna on the south shore. Best overall window for variety.
  • May-July: Offshore transitions to dolphin and blue marlin. Flats fishing remains solid. Water temperature climbs but fish stay active.
  • August-October: Hurricane season. Fishing can be excellent between weather systems, but trip insurance is mandatory. Some charters shut down September and October.

Where to Stay

Pelican Bay Hotel at Lucaya is the most convenient base for both offshore and flats fishing. The marina holds charter boats and has fuel, bait, and tackle. Old Bahama Bay Resort at West End puts you right on top of the wahoo grounds, with a full-service marina and waterfront rooms. For east end bonefishing, Deep Water Cay is a dedicated fly fishing lodge with guided programs and private flats access. In Freeport, budget options are plentiful, and rental cars make the 45-minute drive to West End or the east end straightforward.

Tackle Recommendations

  • Wahoo trolling: 50-80 lb class stand-up combos, high-speed lures, single-strand wire or 480 lb cable, 300+ yards of backing
  • Sailfish: 20-30 lb stand-up, rigged ballyhoo, 7/0 circle hooks, fluorocarbon leaders
  • Yellowfin tuna: 50 lb stand-up or spinning, chunking rigs with 5/0-7/0 circles, butterfly jigs for vertical work
  • Bonefishing (fly): 7-8 wt rod, floating tropical line, #4-6 Gotcha/Crazy Charlie/Spawning Shrimp
  • Bonefishing (spin): Light spinning, 8-10 lb braid, 12 lb fluoro leader, 1/8 oz jigs with shrimp
  • Reef fishing: Medium spinning, 15-20 lb line, 1/0-2/0 circle hooks, cut bait

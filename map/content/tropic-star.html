Tropic Star Lodge sits in Pinas Bay, on the wild Pacific coast of Panama's Darien Province — 100 miles from the nearest road. You fly in on a charter plane, land on a jungle airstrip, and step into one of the most legendary fishing lodges ever built. Zane Grey Reef is 5 miles offshore.

Why Tropic Star

The numbers tell the story: over 300 IGFA world records have been set from this lodge. The Zane Grey Reef — named for the famous author and angler who fished here in the 1930s — is a volcanic seamount that creates a massive upwelling zone. Black marlin, blue marlin, Pacific sailfish, yellowfin tuna, dorado, and roosterfish converge on this structure in staggering numbers.

The lodge has operated since 1962, running a fleet of custom 31-foot Bertrams. It's all-inclusive: flights from Panama City, meals, open bar, and unlimited fishing. Conservation is taken seriously — circle hooks and catch-and-release are standard practice.

How to Fish It

Offshore — Zane Grey Reef

Trolling over and around the reef with a mix of lures and live bait. Black marlin are the marquee catch — fish averaging 300-500 lbs, with 700+ lb fish showing up every season. Blue marlin and striped marlin join the mix, along with massive yellowfin tuna.

Sailfish are thick here from May through August, with 10+ release days common. The boats run heavy tackle for marlin (80-130 lb) and lighter outfits for sails (30-50 lb).

Inshore

Roosterfish along the rocky shoreline are a light-tackle highlight. Live bait (bonito strips, sardines) fished on 20-30 lb spinning tackle produces fish up to 60 lbs. Cubera snapper, grouper, and amberjack fill out the inshore calendar.

When to Go

  • January–February: Peak black marlin season. The biggest fish of the year.
  • March–April: Blue marlin transition. Yellowfin tuna thick on the reef.
  • May–August: Sailfish peak. 10-20 release days. Roosterfish tournament in May.
  • September–December: Mixed bag. Good all-around fishing, fewer anglers.

The Lodge

12 rooms, 12 boats, maximum 24 anglers at a time. It's not cheap — a week runs $6,000-8,000 per person — but it includes everything. The lodge itself is set in a cove surrounded by primary rainforest. Howler monkeys, macaws, and the occasional jaguar track are part of the scenery.

Tackle Recommendations

  • Black marlin: 80-130 lb stand-up (provided by lodge)
  • Sailfish: 30-50 lb conventional, circle hooks, live bait
  • Roosterfish: 20-30 lb spinning, live sardines or bonito strips
  • Tuna: 50-80 lb conventional, chunk bait or trolling feathers

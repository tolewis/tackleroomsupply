Sixty miles east of Miami, Bimini sits right on the edge of the Gulf Stream — one of the most productive fishing corridors in the Atlantic. The deep blue water runs within sight of shore, which means you're on fish fast.

Why Bimini

The Gulf Stream pushes north past Bimini at 3-4 knots, carrying bait and every pelagic species that follows it. Schoolie dolphin (mahi) ride the weed lines from spring through fall. Blue marlin move through from April to September, with the peak bite in June and July. Sailfish show up earlier — February through April — working the same current edges.

On the flats side, Bimini's western shore has some of the best bonefishing in the Bahamas. Guides like Ansil Saunders have been poling those flats for decades. Average bonefish run 3-5 lbs, with shots at double-digit fish on the right tide.

How to Fish It

Offshore

Trolling with ballyhoo and lure spreads is the standard play. Most charters run 6-rod spreads — long riggers, short riggers, flat lines, and a shotgun. For dolphin, keep light spinning tackle rigged with a chunk of ballyhoo or a small jig. When you find a weed line or floating debris, you can cast to them all day.

Blue marlin anglers typically fish 50- to 80-lb stand-up tackle, with a mix of lures and live bait (goggle-eyes, pilchards). Circle hooks are encouraged by most captains for release.

Flats

Bonefishing is primarily sight-casting on foot or from a poled skiff. Fly anglers use 7-8 wt rods with #4-6 Gotcha or Crazy Charlie patterns. Spin anglers do well with 1/8 oz jigs tipped with shrimp. Best tides: incoming on the western flats.

When to Go

  • March–April: Sailfish peak. Bonefish tailing on the flats.
  • May–July: Blue marlin season. Dolphin thick on the weed lines.
  • August–October: Wahoo start showing. Offshore slows slightly but still productive.
  • November–February: Bonefishing remains strong. Fewer offshore trips due to weather windows.

Where to Stay

Bimini Big Game Club Resort & Marina is the hub — full-service marina, tackle shop, restaurant, and a fleet of charter boats. Resorts World Bimini offers a newer option on the south end with a larger marina. For budget, Brown's Marina on North Bimini has slips and simple rooms.

Tackle Recommendations

  • Offshore trolling: 50-80 lb stand-up combos, 6/0-9/0 circle hooks, rigged ballyhoo, skirted lures
  • Dolphin casting: Medium spinning rod, 20 lb braid, small jigs or live pilchards
  • Bonefishing (fly): 7-8 wt rod, floating line, #4-6 Gotcha/Crazy Charlie/Spawning Shrimp
  • Bonefishing (spin): Light spinning, 8-10 lb line, 1/8 oz jigs with shrimp

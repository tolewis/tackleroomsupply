East Coast Species Quick Reference

Red Drum (Redfish)

Range: Mid-Atlantic to Gulf Coast

Season: Spring and fall runs are peak. Puppy drum (under 27") available most of the year inshore.

Tackle: 15–30 lb braid, 20–30 lb fluorocarbon leader, 2/0–5/0 circle hooks. Live mullet, crab, or cut menhaden.

Striped Bass

Range: Maine to the Carolinas

Season: Spring migration (April–June) and fall run (September–November) are the best windows.

Tackle: 20–50 lb braid, 30–50 lb fluorocarbon, 3/0–8/0 circle hooks. Bunker chunks, eels, or large swimbaits.

Flounder (Summer Flounder)

Range: Cape Hatteras to Maine

Season: May through September inshore. Offshore structure fish year-round.

Tackle: 10–20 lb braid, 15–20 lb fluorocarbon, 1/0–3/0 wide-gap hooks. Bucktail jigs tipped with Gulp or live finger mullet.

Spanish Mackerel

Range: Outer Banks south through the Gulf

Season: Late April through October. Follows baitfish migrations north in spring, south in fall.

Tackle: Light spinning, 10–15 lb mono or braid, 20 lb wire or heavy fluoro leader. Spoons, gotcha plugs, or small jigs trolled or cast.

Cobia

Range: Virginia to the Gulf

Season: May–June migration along the Outer Banks and Chesapeake mouth is prime. Sight fishing from boats.

Tackle: Heavy spinning or baitcasting, 30–50 lb braid, 60–80 lb fluorocarbon leader, 5/0–8/0 circle hooks. Eel, crab, or large bucktail jig.

Bluefish

Range: Full East Coast

Season: Spring and fall migrations. Blitzes can happen anywhere there's bait.

Tackle: Medium spinning, 20–30 lb braid, wire leader (they will cut mono). Metal spoons, poppers, or cut bait.

Core Rigs

Carolina Rig (Bottom Fishing)

  • Sliding egg sinker (1–4 oz depending on current) above a barrel swivel
  • 18–36" fluorocarbon leader, 15–30 lb test
  • Circle hook, 2/0–5/0
  • Best for: redfish, flounder, pompano, black drum

Knocker Rig

  • Egg sinker on the main line directly above the hook — no leader gap
  • Sinker "knocks" the hook, stays tight to structure
  • Best for: snook, grouper, structure fishing

Fishfinder Rig

  • Fishfinder slide on main line, connects to pyramid sinker via snap
  • 12–24" leader, large circle hook (6/0–8/0)
  • Surf fishing standard for drum, stripers, sharks

Popping Cork Rig

  • Popping cork, 18–24" leader, 1/0–3/0 hook or small jig head
  • Shrimp, Gulp, or live bait under the cork
  • Best for: speckled trout, redfish in shallow grass

Bucktail Jig

  • 1/4–2 oz bucktail depending on depth and current
  • Tipped with Gulp Shrimp or Berkley Gulp Swimming Mullet
  • Best for: flounder, stripers, weakfish

Essential Saltwater Knots

Palomar Knot

Best all-around hook attachment. Works on braid and mono. Retains close to 100% line strength when tied correctly. Use for hooks, swivels, and snap swivels.

FG Knot

Top choice for braid-to-fluorocarbon leader connection. Very low profile — passes through guides cleanly. Takes practice but worth it for serious fishing.

Albright Knot

Reliable braid-to-leader connection. Easier to tie than the FG. Slightly bulkier but still guide-friendly. Good backup when you need to tie fast.

Bristol Knot

Mono-to-mono or fluoro-to-fluoro connection. Simple and strong. Good for building rigs with multiple segments of leader material.

Haywire Twist

The standard for attaching single-strand wire to hooks and lures. Must finish with a "barrel roll" wrap — never cut wire straight or it will unravel.

Loop Knot (Rapala Knot)

Ties a loop directly to lure eye, giving it free action in the water. Critical for topwater plugs and any hard lure where action matters.

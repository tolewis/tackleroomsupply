Bermuda sits alone in the North Atlantic, 650 miles east of Cape Hatteras. The island perches on an ancient volcanic seamount, and the surrounding banks — Challenger and Argus — create one of the most concentrated blue marlin fisheries in the world.

Why Bermuda

8 World Cup victories and 19 granders. The blue marlin that frequent Bermuda's banks are among the largest in the Atlantic, with 500+ lb fish caught with regularity during peak season. Challenger Bank sits about 14 miles southwest of the island, dropping from 180 feet to thousands in every direction. It's an underwater island unto itself — and the fish know it.

Bermuda also produces excellent wahoo fishing in winter, yellowfin tuna in the spring, and a nearshore fishery for amberjack, almaco jack, and various reef species.

How to Fish It

Blue Marlin

Trolling the edges of Challenger and Argus Banks with lures and live bait. Bermuda boats favor a mix of Kona-style lures and rigged ballyhoo, running 6-rod spreads at 7-9 knots. The bite tends to come on the edge — where the bank drops off into blue water. GPS marks of the ledges are closely guarded.

Tackle: 80-130 lb stand-up or chair, depending on the boat. Bermuda charter captains know their banks intimately and will position the boat on productive contours.

Wahoo

Winter wahoo fishing (November–March) is almost as big a draw as marlin. High-speed trolling with Wahoo Wahoo, sea witches, and rigged ballyhoo at 12-15 knots picks up 30-70 lb fish. The reef edges and banks hold them.

When to Go

  • May–June: Early marlin. Yellowfin tuna on the banks.
  • July–September: Peak blue marlin season. Tournament circuit in full swing.
  • November–March: Wahoo season. The winter bite can produce 10+ fish days.
  • Year-round: Reef fishing for snappers, jacks, and grouper within 2 miles of shore.

Where to Stay

Royal Naval Dockyard on the west end has the main marina and charter fleet. Hamilton has additional berths. Bermuda is a 2-hour flight from most East Coast airports. The island is expensive — plan accordingly. Charter boats run $1,800-2,500/day for a full offshore trip.

Tackle Recommendations

  • Blue marlin: 80-130 lb tackle (provided by charter), Kona-style lures + rigged ballyhoo
  • Wahoo: 30-50 lb conventional, high-speed lures, wire or heavy fluro leader
  • Reef: 20-30 lb spinning or conventional, live bait or jigs, 40 lb fluro leader

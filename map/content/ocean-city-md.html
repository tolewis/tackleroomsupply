Ocean City, Maryland — the White Marlin Capital of the World. That's not marketing hype. The town hosts the White Marlin Open, the largest and richest billfish tournament on the planet, with purses regularly exceeding $6 million. And the fishing backs it up.

Why Ocean City

The offshore canyons — Washington, Norfolk, Baltimore, Poor Man's — sit 50-80 miles out, right where the continental shelf drops into deep blue water. The Gulf Stream's warm eddies push up against the shelf edge, creating temperature breaks that concentrate white marlin, blue marlin, yellowfin tuna, bigeye tuna, and longfin tuna in a relatively compact zone.

OC has one of the largest charter fleets on the East Coast, with boats ranging from 35-foot center consoles to 60-foot sportfishers. The inlet is easy — no treacherous bar to cross — and the run to the canyons is straight shot east.

How to Fish It

White Marlin

Trolling with ballyhoo — naked or skirted — is the primary method. White marlin are aggressive feeders that respond well to surface presentations. Standard OC spread: long riggers with skirted ballyhoo, short riggers with naked baits, and a flat line with a dredge teaser. When whites are behind the baits, you'll see them light up on the surface.

Tackle: 20-30 lb stand-up, circle hooks (required in tournaments). Whites average 50-70 lbs but fight like fish twice their size.

Tuna

Yellowfin tuna on the chunk is a night-bite specialty. Boats anchor on the shelf edge with a chum slick of butterfish and sardines, then fish chunks on 50 lb tackle. Bigeye tuna to 200+ lbs hit in the same zone. Daytime trolling with spreader bars, cedar plugs, and skirted ballyhoo picks up yellowfin and longfin on the troll.

Inshore

The bays behind OC hold flounder, rockfish (striped bass), bluefish, and tautog. The Route 50 bridge is a productive night spot for stripers. Surf fishing on Assateague Island (just south of the inlet) produces red drum, bluefish, and kingfish in the fall run.

When to Go

  • June: Early season. Yellowfin tuna firing. White marlin starting to show.
  • July–August: Peak white marlin. White Marlin Open in August. Blue marlin in the mix.
  • September: Bigeye tuna and wahoo. Excellent offshore variety.
  • October–November: Fall run — stripers, bluefish, red drum in the surf.

Where to Stay

The marinas cluster around the inlet: Ocean City Fishing Center, Sunset Marina, and Harbour Island Marina all have charter fleets. The boardwalk and downtown strip have hundreds of hotels and rental condos. Book early for White Marlin Open week.

Tackle Recommendations

  • White marlin: 20-30 lb stand-up, skirted ballyhoo, circle hooks
  • Tuna (chunk): 50-80 lb conventional, butterfish chunks, fluorocarbon leader
  • Tuna (troll): 30-50 lb, spreader bars, cedar plugs, skirted ballyhoo
  • Surf: 10-12' heavy surf rod, 4-8 oz sinker, cut bait (mullet, bunker, clam)

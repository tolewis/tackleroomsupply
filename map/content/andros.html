Andros is the largest island in the Bahamas and the undisputed bonefish capital of the world. The western shore is lined with miles of pristine flats — wadeable hard sand and turtle grass that hold more bonefish per acre than anywhere else in the Caribbean.

Why Andros

The numbers are simple: Andros has more bonefish on more accessible flats than any other destination in the world. Average fish run 3-5 lbs, with legitimate shots at 8-10 lb trophies on most trips. The island's western shelf is a vast expanse of shallow water — the "tongue of the ocean" drops to 6,000 feet just east of the island, pushing nutrient-rich water onto the flats and feeding the entire food chain.

Beyond bones, Andros offers permit on the deeper flats, barracuda in the channels, and occasional tarpon in the creek mouths. The east side has deep-water access for offshore trips, though most anglers come here for one thing: sight-casting to tailing bonefish.

How to Fish It

Bonefishing

Poled skiff on the flats, sight-casting to singles and small schools. The standard approach: guide on the platform, angler on the bow, scanning the water for tails, muds, or nervous water. When you see them, you've got 30-50 feet to make your cast count.

  • Fly: 7-8 wt rod, floating tropical line, 10-12' leader to 12 lb tippet. Gotcha, Crazy Charlie, Spawning Shrimp, or Bonefish Bitters in #4-6
  • Spin: Light spinning rod, 8-10 lb braid with 12 lb fluro leader, 1/8 oz jighead with shrimp or small soft plastic

Best tides: incoming and first hour of outgoing. The fish push up onto the flats to feed as the water rises, then retreat to deeper channels as it drops.

Permit & Tarpon

Permit are present on the deeper sand flats and around coral heads. Live crabs are the go-to bait on spin tackle; fly anglers throw Merkin or Raghead crab patterns on 9-10 wt rods. Tarpon show in the creek mouths and deeper channels, especially during the spring spawn run.

When to Go

  • October–May: Prime bonefishing season. Cooler water concentrates fish on the flats.
  • March–April: Permit start showing on the flats. Tarpon in the creek mouths.
  • June–September: Summer is fishable but hot. Afternoon thunderstorms are daily. Bonefish are still there but can be scattered in the heat.

Where to Stay

Small Hope Bay Lodge on the north end is the original — a low-key dive and fishing lodge since 1960. Swain's Cay Lodge on South Andros is purpose-built for fly anglers. Bair's Lodge on South Andros is another top option with guided flats programs. Fresh Creek (Andros Town) has a few smaller lodges and independent guides.

Tackle Recommendations

  • Bonefishing (fly): 7-8 wt rod, floating line, #4-6 Gotcha/Crazy Charlie/Spawning Shrimp
  • Bonefishing (spin): Ultralight to light spinning, 8-10 lb line, 1/8 oz jigs with shrimp
  • Permit: 9-10 wt fly or medium spinning, live crabs, Merkin crab patterns
  • General: Polarized sunglasses are non-negotiable. Amber or copper lenses for the flats
